In the current global digital world, customer personalization and social media analysis have become essential for operating a business within the e-commerce sector. Which is why data analytics and machine learning initiatives have become vital for tailoring marketing and product offerings to known and unknown customer bases.

By analyzing social media data and customer behavior, machine learning algorithms can create effective and detailed customer segmentation models that group customers based on common characteristics such as demographic, buying behavior, and website interactions.

Due to the increasingly highly competitive landscape of the e-commerce industry, businesses are constantly looking for ways to deliver a personalized customer experience without downplaying the importance of data privacy and transparency.

One of the most effective ways of achieving personalized marketing experiences is by the use of machine learning and data analytics applications which use data such as demographics, purchase history and interactions within social media.

Utilizing social media data can be extremely useful in order to identify patterns and trends in customer behavior, by identifying which products are more likely to be of interest to a certain group of customers, allowing the business to promote social media initiatives that positively impact product sales and customer satisfaction.

Since social media has grown to be the main approach that businesses use as a means to connect with different customer bases. By analyzing social media data through different types of statistical tests, businesses can generate effective customer personalization strategies that depend heavily on the accessibility and quality of the data that is being analyzed.

Within this project, we'll seek machine learning algorithms that uses customer data as a way to personalize marketing strategies and product recommendations by creating customer groups through classification models. As well as, using social media data to gain insights about customer opinions, preferences, and behaviors in order to shape marketing efforts.

This project handles the objectives of: Customer personalization and Social media analysis by using simulated data that might be found in a typical e-commerce company that handles luxury fashion products. (As a result, all the data presented in this project is simulated and is not representative of real-world data)

STEP 1: Business Understanding

I. Customer personalization:
Customer personalization is an essential process within marketing as it is involved with customer segmentation through grouping based on data. This process seeks to adapt marketing strategies to ever-changing customer preferences and behaviors.

II. Social media analysis:
Social Media Analysis uses the information taken from social media and apply statistical tests in order to evaluate the effectiveness of their strategies and make data-driven decisions for improvement. Like identifying customer engagement rate, customer retention rate, conversion rate, etc.

For Key Performance Indicators (KPI) we'll take into consideration the value added to the financial returns from investment in customer personalization and social media analysis strategies. In short, the ONLY KPI that should be considered after applying a marketing project is the Return of Investment (ROI)

STEP 2: Data Understanding

Data understanding is focused on covering all activities that may be involved with the problem. In the case for our marketing project, we focus on simply collecting data from the e-commerce platform and analytics obtained from the social media platforms.

Within the e-commerce platform, the website can collect demographic features, purchase history, and website interactions as well as other behavioral features like (visit times, bargain-seekers, wandering customers, impulse buyers, need-based shoppers, and loyal customers)

On the other hand, we will take into consideration data from social media platforms by tracking online interactions with customers such as marketing campaigns, types of content, engagement with customers, and brand relevance.

NOTE: Within this project we won't consider any data quality issues like anomalies or missing data as all data is generated specifically for this project.
